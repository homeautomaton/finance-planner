Financial Forecast

Financial Forecast is an interactive long-term money simulation and planning tool. It combines retirement forecasting, cash-flow planning, savings visualization, and “what-if” experimentation into a single graph-driven interface.

The application is designed both as a learning tool for younger users trying to understand the long-term effects of income and spending habits, and as a practical planning tool for later-life forecasting and retirement preparation.

Interactive Forecasting

Purpose of the Application

Most financial decisions do not feel important in the moment. The purpose of Financial Forecast is to make those long-term consequences visible.

The app focuses on one core question:

“If I continue along this path, where does it eventually lead?”

Rather than concentrating on investment complexity, the simulator emphasizes:

  • Income versus spending
  • Long-term consistency
  • The effects of inflation
  • Lifestyle growth over time
  • The value of savings habits
  • The danger of persistent overspending
The simulator intentionally stays relatively simple. It is designed to encourage understanding and experimentation, not to replace professional financial software or tax planning.

What the Graph Represents

The chart displays three independent lines over time:

Line Description
Balance (Blue) Your projected account balance or net savings over time. This is affected by investment return assumptions, salary, spending, windfalls, and planned expenses.
Salary (Green) Your projected after-tax income. Salary can automatically grow annually using the Raises % setting, or be manually overridden for specific years.
Spending (Red) Your yearly spending or living costs. Spending typically rises due to inflation, but may also be manually adjusted.

Core Assumptions

The left-side control panel contains the global assumptions used by the forecast engine.

Setting Meaning
Starting Balance Your initial savings or account balance.
Birth Year Used to calculate your age at each point in the timeline.
Years How many future years should be simulated.
Return % Assumed annual investment growth rate.
Inflation % Automatic yearly growth of spending.
Raises % Automatic yearly growth of salary.
Salary after taxes Starting yearly income after taxes.
Spending Starting yearly spending.

Interactive Editing

One of the application's most important features is direct manipulation of the chart.

Dragging Points

  • Click and drag a point to change its value directly on the graph.
  • While dragging, values normally snap to the nearest 100 units for cleaner editing.
  • Hold Shift while dragging to disable snapping.
  • Dragging creates a persistent override for that year.

Opening the Year Editor

  • Click a point without dragging to open the detailed year editor.
  • The year editor allows multiple changes at once.

Removing Overrides

  • Hold Alt / Option and click a point to remove its saved override.
The graph is designed to encourage experimentation. Try intentionally unrealistic scenarios just to understand how sensitive long-term outcomes are to relatively small yearly changes.

The Year Editor

Clicking a year opens a detailed editor for that specific point in time.

Overrides

Overrides replace the simulator's calculated value for that year.

Field Purpose
Balance Replace the calculated balance with an exact value.
Salary Override income beginning that year.
Spending Override yearly spending beginning that year.

One-Time Events

Field Purpose
Windfall Income Add a one-time increase to the balance. Examples: inheritance, selling a house, bonuses, business sale, lawsuit settlement.
Planned Expense Add a one-time reduction in balance. Examples: home purchase, vehicle replacement, surgery, tuition, remodeling.

JSON Scenario System

Every scenario in the app is represented as JSON.

The JSON panel is not merely an export format — it is the application's complete internal state.

Why This Matters

  • Scenarios are easy to save and share.
  • Power users can edit values directly.
  • Forecasts are transparent and inspectable.
  • No hidden database or backend exists.

Example

{
  "startingBalance": 15000,
  "startingSalary": 45000,
  "startingSpending": 32000,

  "salaryAdjustments": {
    "2030": 60000
  },

  "plannedExpenses": {
    "2032": 18000
  },

  "windfallIncome": {
    "2040": 250000
  }
}

Retirement Planning Uses

The simulator works well as a lightweight retirement planner.

Common uses include:

  • Testing retirement ages
  • Estimating sustainable spending
  • Modeling reduced post-retirement income
  • Planning major purchases
  • Visualizing investment growth over decades
  • Exploring “coast FIRE” style scenarios
  • Evaluating the impact of lifestyle inflation
Because the simulator is intentionally simple, it should be viewed as a directional planning tool rather than a precise predictor.

Educational Uses

Financial Forecast is especially effective as a teaching tool because it makes compounding visually obvious.

Younger users can quickly discover:

  • Small spending increases become large long-term costs.
  • Early savings matter more than many people expect.
  • Raises alone do not guarantee wealth accumulation.
  • Inflation silently changes future spending requirements.
  • A modest positive yearly gap between income and spending can compound dramatically over time.

Important Limitations

This is not professional financial software.

The application intentionally avoids many forms of complexity in order to remain understandable and interactive.

Current Simplifications

  • No debt interest calculations
  • No loan amortization
  • No tax modeling
  • No account type distinctions
  • No inflation-adjusted investment classes
  • No Social Security calculations
  • No Monte Carlo simulation
  • No sequence-of-returns analysis

Debt Modeling

Debt is intentionally simplified.

If you want to simulate debt repayment, debt payments should generally be included in yearly spending.

However, because debt interest does not compound separately, the simulator becomes less accurate for highly leveraged situations.

The application is fundamentally optimized around:

  • Positive savings behavior
  • Long-term investing
  • Cash-flow awareness
  • Living below income

The “Dysentery” Event

If the projected balance falls below the application's hard debt threshold, the simulator displays the “You have died of dysentery” overlay.

This is intentionally humorous and acts as a visual warning that the simulated financial path has become catastrophically unsustainable.

Clicking the overlay minimizes it.

Suggested Workflow

  1. Enter realistic starting values.
  2. Adjust expected raises and inflation.
  3. Experiment with spending levels.
  4. Add major future purchases.
  5. Model career changes or retirement.
  6. Compare multiple saved scenarios.
  7. Observe which variables matter most.
Most users discover that spending consistency matters more than isolated large events.

Design Philosophy

Financial software often becomes so detailed that users stop experimenting.

Financial Forecast intentionally favors:

  • Simplicity
  • Immediate visual feedback
  • Rapid experimentation
  • Human readability
  • Transparency of assumptions

The goal is not perfect prediction. The goal is improved intuition.
